About Me

As a UX Designer with a background in multimedia design, I possess a natural curiosity and a strong drive to learn new technologies. My passion lies in product design, and I am committed to finding meaningful solutions to problems that positively impact the quality of life for the users I design for.

I have been a designer and creator of effective corporate branding campaigns across all media platforms, and I am experienced in the design and creation of logos, layouts, videos, animation, interactive media and user interfaces. I attained expertise in designing and refreshing impactful branding across all functions of an organization with a holistic approach for targeted audiences. I partner cross-functionally and operate effectively in environments with a global reach.

Through my passion for design I seek to combine my skills and experiences and expand my world in more useful ways. Being a part of a team of like minded individuals that strive to find design solutions and solve problems that have a positive impact on our way of life, our environment and the ways in which we communicate with each other is important to me. Creating designs that enhance the experience of an audience by making their life easier and helping them navigate their world with honesty and true understanding feed my curiosity and fuel my passion for design.

My Pavlos Savva, Παύλος Σάββα is Greek. I am from the Island of Cyprus. My hometown, the beautiful Mediterranean city of Larnaca is a place of rich history and 4000 years of continuous habitation.

Growing up in Larnaca, I was part of the community through my involvement in the Sea scouts. There I acquired a multitude of life skills and experienced life in very unique ways, through the scouts group's activities that were filled with adventures and giving back to the community. My early fascination with design involved taking apart my toys to explore how they worked. I often built different functions out of left over parts. My curiosity for radio control airplanes, combined with the scarcity of funds for the hobby, led me to scratch build my own airplane designs, some of which flew better than others.

At the age of 19, I moved to Tucson where I studied at the University of Arizona. I followed an interdisciplinary program in multimedia and information design that provided me with the unique opportunity to build the structure of my studies based on my interests while working closely with an advisor who guided and supported me throughout.
