Museum Aid

Description: Museum Aid is a brain child of mine, for an app that can make any museum anywhere in the world accessible to any person with access to a smartphone.

Problem Hypothesis: A virtual reality platform specializing in immersive experiences for museums around the world is necessary to provide educators, parents, and disabled persons with an alternative option to museum-going.

Project Team: Pavlos Savva (PM), Nancy Rodriguez, Florence Ellison, Maddie Dewitt

Tools used: Trello, Miro, Figma, FigJam, Photoshop, Illustrator, G-Suite

User Research

As a team we developed a user interview plan and an online survey in order to to get enough qualitative and quantitative data to validate our problem hypothesis. We performed 7 interviews, and I had the opportunity to conduct 2 of them. Also, 44 people answered our survey. One interesting finding was that people with kids rated their museum experience at just 5.5 out of 10.

The Insights from this research helped us define our user, the actual problem, and also the value proposition for our product.

We discovered that many families and educators are looking for a more enriching trip to museums around the world with no geographical constraints.

Definition

Problem Statement: Sofia Perez, a mother of two, requires an easy and inexpensive way to access interactive and educational museum experiences from around the world for her children.

Value Proposition: We are developing a way to provide virtual museum tours to help users gain access to museums without any geographical or financial limitations.

We include each piece of art interactively, this will allow the user gain a more immersive and enjoyable experience than they would at a physical museum.

User Persona

Empathy Map

Our user, Sofia Perez! She wants to visit museums and take her family, but having little kids and being in a remote location can make that a frustrating trip instead of an enjoyable one.

Ideation

Sofia's main goal is wanting to find a way to bring the museum to her home with her family. When she downloads the app, she is very intrigued and interested once she logs in with everything the app has to offer.. She is excited to pick from any museum around the world. She feels extremely relieved and happy when she decides to choose the Smithsonian American Art Museum and purchases her experience.

Our opportunities focus on creating subscriptions, discounts days and each child having their own user page.

Usability Testing

We performed Guerilla usability testing with 6 users. I performed 2 of those tests.

Test 1:Create an account.
User's showed hesitation when trying to create an account.

Task 2:Select a museum from a category.
This was fairly easy for most users.

Task 3:Preview a VR experience.
There was no back button in the first prototype, which confused users.

Task 4:Purchase a Museum VR.
The user was expecting additional steps after selecting the purchase button on the app

Test 1 changes

  • Replaced ‘Continue’ to ‘Start’
  • Highlighted sign in process
  • Sign up changed to ‘Create Account.’
  • Removed the Phone requirement when trying to create an account.

Test 2 changes

  • Added the search screen to the homescreen

Test 1 changes

  • Added a welcome message in the VR screen
  • Added a ‘Back’ button to return and facilitate navigation

Test 1 changes

  • Adjusted the menu bar to include a purchased tickets button
  • Added a confirmation step for purchases
